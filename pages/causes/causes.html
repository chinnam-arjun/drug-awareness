Risk & Protective Factors

Risk Factors
  • Early exposure to substance use
  • Trauma or abuse history
  • Untreated mental health issues
  • Academic failure or disengagement
  • Family history of addiction
  • Poor supervision or lack of boundaries
Protective Factors
  • Strong family support and communication
  • Positive peer group & role models
  • Good coping skills & problem‚Äësolving
  • Involvement in sports, arts, volunteering
  • School connectedness & achievement
  • Access to counseling & helplines
Tip: Strengthening protective factors can reduce risk even when some risk factors are present. Early prevention works best.
