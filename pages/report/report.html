Reasons for Drug Addiction

  • By Parenting – Lack of guidance, neglect, or exposure to drug use in the family can influence children.
  • By Friends Forcing – Peer pressure is one of the leading causes of first-time drug usage among teens.
  • Curiosity – The urge to try new experiences can push young individuals toward experimenting with drugs.
  • Stress or Depression – Emotional struggles often lead individuals to seek temporary relief through substances.
  • Media Influence – Movies, music, or social media portraying drugs as “cool” can normalize their use.
  • Easy Availability – When drugs are easily accessible, people are more likely to try them.

Conclusion

Based on this survey, Friends Forcing emerged as the most common reason for drug addiction among students. Peer influence can be extremely strong during teenage years, often overpowering personal judgment. Educating students about resisting peer pressure and promoting healthy friendships is essential in prevention efforts.
