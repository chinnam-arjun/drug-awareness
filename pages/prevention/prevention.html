Core Prevention Strategies

📚
Education

Provide age‑appropriate, factual information about drugs, risks, and consequences—at home and in school.

🧠
Life Skills

Build refusal skills, stress‑management, decision‑making, and problem‑solving to handle pressure.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦
Family Bonds

Encourage open conversations, clear rules, consistent supervision, and supportive relationships.

🏫
School & Community

Create safe spaces, positive activities, and quick access to counseling and helplines.

Tip: Prevention works best when schools, families, and communities deliver the same message—early and consistently.

Refusal Skills — Handling Peer Pressure

🛑
Simple Scripts to Say "No"
  • “No thanks, I don’t do that.”
  • “I’m in training / I’ve got early practice.”
  • “Not my thing—I’m good.”
  • “I promised my parents/coach I’d stay clean.”
  • “I want to remember tonight clearly.”
🧭
Smart Tactics
  • Change the subject or suggest another activity.
  • Use the buddy system—stick with drug‑free friends.
  • Plan exits: have a ride, money, or a trusted contact.
  • Leave if you feel unsafe. Your safety comes first.
Practicing these lines out loud builds confidence. Role‑play in class or at home.

Healthy Alternatives to Stay Drug‑Free

⚽
Sports & Fitness

Join teams, try new sports, or build a routine—movement reduces stress and boosts mood.

🎨
Arts & Hobbies

Music, dance, coding, photography, volunteering—discover passions that give purpose.

🧘
Mind & Well‑being

Practice mindfulness, journaling, or yoga. Sleep well and eat balanced meals.

Everyone Has a Role

👪
Parents & Caregivers
  • Talk early and often; listen without judgment.
  • Set clear rules and follow through consistently.
  • Know your child’s friends and where they are.
  • Model healthy coping and substance‑free behavior.
👩‍🏫
Teachers & Schools
  • Integrate prevention into curricula and clubs.
  • Identify students at risk and refer early.
  • Create safe, inclusive, and engaged classrooms.
  • Partner with counselors and local NGOs.
🤝
Friends & Peers
  • Support each other’s goals; don’t pressure.
  • Speak up if you’re worried—tell a trusted adult.
  • Share accurate info; avoid harmful myths.
  • Celebrate drug‑free choices together.

Personal Prevention Checklist

My Commitments
  • I choose drug‑free lifestyle and friends.
  • I practice refusal lines and exit plans.
  • I manage stress with healthy habits.
  • I ask for help when I need it.
My Support Network
  • Two trusted adults I can call anytime.
  • Peer group that respects my choices.
  • Local resources/helplines saved on phone.
  • Weekly activity that keeps me engaged.
Good to know: Small, consistent steps beat one‑time big efforts. Keep practicing your plan.
